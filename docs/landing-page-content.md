# Hybro — Landing Page Content & Design Brief

Use this document to design and build the Hybro landing page. This is a **mobile app landing page** — the product is an iOS app (coming soon to the App Store). The landing page advertises the app, shows mockups of the mobile UI, and drives users to join the waitlist. This is NOT a web app landing page.

---

## Brand Identity

**App Name:** Hybro
**Platform:** iOS (coming soon to the App Store)
**Tagline:** Your AI fitness coach that sees everything.
**One-liner:** Hybro connects your fitness apps, analyzes your data, and coaches you — 24/7.

### Logo Instructions

Generate a modern, minimal logo for "Hybro" — an AI fitness coaching platform for serious athletes.

**Requirements:**
- The wordmark "Hybro" should be clean, bold, and modern — think geometric sans-serif (similar to Inter Black, Satoshi Bold, or General Sans)
- Include an icon/logomark that can stand alone. It should subtly reference both strength and endurance (hybrid athlete concept). Ideas: an abstract dumbbell + running stride merged, a stylized "H" with forward motion, or a minimal figure that suggests both lifting and running
- Primary color palette: deep black (#0A0A0A) background with an electric accent — either electric blue (#3B82F6), vivid green (#22C55E), or hot orange (#F97316). Pick one accent and commit
- The logo should work on both dark and light backgrounds
- No gradients, no 3D effects, no generic "brain" or "robot" AI imagery
- It should feel like a premium fitness brand (think Whoop, NOBULL, Hyperice), not a tech startup

## Feature Sections

**Headline:** Tell us your goals. We'll build your plan.

**Body:** Whether you're training for an Ironman or just trying to build muscle, Hybro's onboarding asks the right questions and generates a personalized training split in under 3 minutes.

**Details to show:**

---

### Feature 2: Unified Dashboard

**Headline:** Everything you need. One glance.

**Body:** Your dashboard shows today's session, this week's progress, and your key metrics — all pulled from your connected apps in real time.

**Details to show:**
- Today's session card ("Push Day" with AI notes like "keep it light, HRV was low")
- Weekly strip (Mon-Sun showing session types and completion status)
- Quick stats: calories today vs target, weight trend, sleep/HRV snapshot
- Sync status for each connected app

---

### Feature 3: AI Coach Chat

**Headline:** Ask anything. Get answers that know your data.

**Body:** Hybro's AI coach isn't a generic chatbot. It has access to your actual nutrition, workouts, recovery, and training plan. Ask it what to eat for dinner, whether you should train today, or how to fix a lagging body part — and get advice based on your real numbers.

**Example conversations to show:**
- "What should I eat for dinner?" → Checks today's macros, sees 80g protein left, suggests high-protein meals
- "I feel beat up, should I train today?" → Checks HRV (low), sleep (5.5h), recent volume (high) → recommends a rest day
- "My shoulders are lagging" → Suggests switching from PPL to Arnold split or adding lateral raise volume
- "Add a swim session this week" → Checks calendar availability, proposes Thursday 6 AM

---

### Feature 4: Auto-Adjusting Training Plan

**Headline:** Your plan evolves with you. Every week.

**Body:** Every week, Hybro reviews your training compliance, nutrition, recovery trends, and performance. It suggests adjustments — and you approve them before anything changes.

**Details to show:**
- Weekly review card with compliance %, nutrition stats, recovery trends
- AI-generated adjustment suggestions with reasoning
- "Approve / Modify / Reject" controls
- Example adjustments:
  - "You missed 2 of 4 sessions → reducing frequency, keeping intensity"
  - "HRV trending down + poor sleep → inserting extra rest day"
  - "4 weeks from race → beginning taper phase"

## Design Direction

**Overall feel:** Premium, dark-mode-first, minimal. Think Whoop's marketing site meets Linear's clean aesthetic. No clutter, no stock photos of gyms, no generic fitness imagery.

**Typography:** Clean geometric sans-serif. Inter, Satoshi, or General Sans for body. Tighter, bolder weight for headings.

**Color palette:**
- Background: deep black (#0A0A0A) or very dark gray (#111111)
- Text: white (#FAFAFA) with gray (#A1A1AA) for secondary
- Accent: one strong color — electric blue (#3B82F6), vivid green (#22C55E), or hot orange (#F97316)
- Cards/surfaces: subtle dark gray (#1A1A1A) with soft borders (#262626)

**Layout:**
- Full-width sections with generous whitespace
- Centered content, max-width ~1200px
- Alternating left/right feature layouts or centered with mockup below
- Sticky navigation with logo + CTA

**Animations:**
- Subtle scroll-triggered fade-ins
- Integration logos with a "connect" animation (lines drawing between them)
- Dashboard mockup with live-feeling data transitions
- No heavy animations, no parallax — keep it fast and clean

**Do NOT:**
- Use stock photos of people in gyms
- Use generic AI imagery (robots, brains, neural networks)
- Use gradients everywhere
- Make it look like a SaaS dashboard template or web app
- Use Comic Sans or playful fonts
- Show desktop/browser mockups — this is a mobile app

**DO:**
- Make it feel like a premium fitness brand
- Use iPhone mockups throughout to showcase the app UI
- Keep copy short and punchy
- Let whitespace do the work
- Make the waitlist CTA impossible to miss
- Include "Coming Soon to iOS" messaging prominently in hero and footer
- Use App Store badge (grayed out / coming soon variant)
- Show the app in context — a phone in hand, on a gym bench, or floating with accent glow
- Feature sections should each have a phone mockup showing that specific screen (dashboard, chat, plan view, weekly review, integrations)
